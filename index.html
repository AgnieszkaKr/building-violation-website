The New York City Housing Maintenance Code establishes the minimum standards for health and safety, fire protection, light, and ventilation cleanliness, repair and maintenance, and occupancy in residential dwellings. In accordance with the Code, the Department of Housing Preservation and Development (HPD) issues violations against conditions, in rental dwelling units and buildings, that have been verified to violate the New York City Housing Maintenance Code (HMC) or the New York State Multiple Dwelling Law (MDL). Violations are registered by Housing Inspectors after a physical inspection of a building. (except for class I violations which are generally administratively issued). Violations are categorized into four classes: I violations which are generally administratively issued). Violations are categorized into four classes:
  • A (non-hazardous),
  • Class B (hazardous),
  • Class C (immediately hazardous),
  • Class I (information orders).
